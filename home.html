Holograms

A Look Into Holography

What are Holograms?

Holograms are a way of displaying 3-D images in real life, but that defnition alone doesn't do it justice.

You might know them from Sci-Fi movies or novels, as they have been heavily popularized in Science Fiction culture since the 20th century. It's led many people to wonder if these 3-dimensional displays of light are just pigments of our imaginations. Although most examples in both film and video games are pretty unrealistic, it's inspired many innovations such as in the tech field of Augmented Reality (AR), probably giving us the closest thing we have to holograms yet (although not technically in "real life").

The Different Uses of Holography

There have been a wide variety of speculated uses for Holography, most notably in security, and even artist work.

The 70's and 80's were a great time for attempted uses of holography in the field of art. Although most failed, artists such as Matt Brand still integrate those elements in the work today, mainly using specular holography, which controls the motion of "specular glints" on a 2-D surface. There's also optical holography, which uses an optical wavefront that is recorded and then re-constucted after. In security, to protect indentities and to identify counterfeits from real ones, it uses pictorial and textual elements, and in currency it is also prominent, being used in cash, credit cards and bank cards, and can even be found in passports.
